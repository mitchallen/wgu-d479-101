Welcome to Taniti!

The island of Taninti is a tropical paradise in the South Pacific.

Come experience our warm crystal-clear waters and sandy beaches. Explore our lush rainforests, and marvel at our majestic mountains, including an active volcano.

Whether you on your honeymoon, taking a family vacation or an outdoor adventurer, our island has something to offer for everyone.

Things to Do

Sightseeing

Tourists spend most of their time in Taniti City, which boasts native architecture and nearby white, sandy beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay. Other popular activities include boat or bus tours of the island, hikes in the rainforest, or visits to Taniti’s active volcano.

Beachgoers

Beachgoers can find umbrellas and chairs for rent at most beaches. Some hotels will even provide towels. They will even bring you drinks and food if you ask.

The most popular beaches are:

  • Merriton Landing
  • Fantasy Island
  • Matagi Bay

Food & Drink

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants: five serve mostly local fish and rice, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.

The drinking age on Taniti is 18 and the drinking age is not strictly enforced.

The most popular restaurants are:

  • The Beachcomber - Local seafood
    Hours: Daily 11am - 10pm, Happy Hour 3pm - 6pm
  • Fin's Finest - Pan-Asian fusion
    Hours: Tues-Sun 5pm - 11pm, Closed Mondays
  • The Crabby Shack - Casual American & seafood
    Hours: Daily 7am - 9pm, Breakfast served until 11am

Holidays

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Currency

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.

Lodging

Taniti has a wide variety of lodging that ranges from an inexpensive hostel to one large, four-star resort. There are many small, family-owned hotels and a growing number of bed and breakfasts. All types of lodging are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government.

Power outlets in most resorts and hotels are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

Getting Around

Language

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents. If you are lost in the city, just ask anyone for directions and they will be more than happy to help you.

Ground

Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. every day.

For $5 USD you can buy a bus pass from the driver and ride the bus all day.

Private buses serve the rest of the island.

Taxis are available in Taniti City, and rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport.

Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors (helmets are required by law).

Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay in the area surrounding Merriton Landing: this area is easy to explore on foot.

Airport

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air. Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Taniti is in the process of expanding the airport so larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years.

Airport Information:

  • Location: 5 miles north of Taniti City
  • Address: 789 Airport Road, Taniti
  • Code: TNT

Getting to/from the airport:
  • Shuttle Service:
    Airport shuttles run every 30 minutes to major hotels
    Cost: $15 per person
  • Taxi:
    Available 24/7 outside the terminal
    Approximate fare to Taniti City: $25-30
  • Public Bus:
    Route 1 connects the airport to downtown
    Runs every hour from 6am to 10pm
    Cost: $3 one-way
  • Car Rental:
    Available at the terminal
    Advance booking recommended during peak season

Cruise

Some visitors arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week.

The cruise ship dock is located at: 123 Main Street, Taniti City.

Safety

Generally you are safe on Taniti. But please keep your valuable close to you.

Hospital

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.
